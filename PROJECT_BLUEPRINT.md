# 🛰️ PROJECT BLUEPRINT: DIY CUBESAT/CANSAT & ROCKET SYSTEM

## 📍 CURRENT PROJECT PROGRESS & STACK

### Phase 1: Core Flight Computer & Wireless Telemetry (COMPLETED)

* **Objective:** Establish the primary "brain" of the spacecraft and construct a localized wireless ground-station communication architecture.
* **Core Hardware:** NodeMCU (ESP8266EX microcontroller) utilizing built-in Wi-Fi and an onboard Active-LOW signaling LED.
* **Current Status:** Successfully compiled and flashed via Arduino IDE over `COM3` using `esptool.py`. The flight computer is fully functional and operates as an independent local Wi-Fi Access Point (SSID: `Satellite-Brain-01`, Gateway: `192.168.4.1`). It hosts a custom localized HTTP web server broadcasting a real-time HTML "Mission Control" telemetry dashboard directly to ground station receivers (smartphones and laptops) without requiring any external internet infrastructure.

## 🛠️ ACTIVE DEVELOPMENT STAGE

### Phase 2: Sensor Integration (Environmental & Orientation Senses)

* **Objective:** Replace static, simulated telemetry parameters on the Mission Control dashboard with live, real-world physical tracking data.
* **Core Hardware Components:**
  * **MPU6050 IMU:** A 3-axis gyroscope and micro-accelerometer to monitor pitch, roll, yaw, and rotational forces.
  * **BMP280 Sensor:** A high-precision barometric altimeter to read ambient atmospheric pressure and temperature, allowing the processor to calculate vertical altitude.
  * **MicroSD Card Module:** A dedicated, localized SPI flash memory module acting as a hardened "Black Box" flight data recorder to safely log all time-stamped telemetry.
* **Current Implementation Goals:** Connect modules over the $I^2C$ communication protocol (SDA to Pin D2, SCL to Pin D1 on NodeMCU). Update the central server loop to read sensor data dynamically and stream it continuously via Wi-Fi to the ground station web application.

## 🚀 UPCOMING DEVELOPMENT PHASES

### Phase 3: Navigation, Power Management & Recovery Tracking

* **Objective:** Ensure completely untethered mobile operation and establish a reliable long-range tracking system for field retrieval.
* **Core Hardware Components:**
  * **NEO-6M GPS Module:** Receives high-altitude satellite arrays to compute active global coordinates (Latitude and Longitude).
  * **3.7V LiPo Battery (1000mAh+) & TP4056 USB Charger:** A dedicated, lightweight, rechargeable energy system built to provide constant current.
  * **GSM Module (SIM800L):** A small cellular transceiver configured to run exclusively during the final retrieval sequence.
* **System Logic Architecture:**
  1. While the satellite is flying in the upper atmosphere, long-range cellular tracking is offline due to a lack of upright cell towers at high altitudes (the "Dead Zone"). The satellite relies entirely on its localized Wi-Fi and internal MicroSD recording.
  2. Once the system enters a parachute descent back to Earth and drops under 2-3 km, the GSM module reconnects to terrestrial networks. Upon touchdown, it automatically sends an SMS text message containing the exact landing GPS coordinates straight to the user's mobile phone.

### Phase 4: Structural Architecture & Thermal Engineering

* **Objective:** Construct a physically sound, completely integrated, and heavily insulated hull to survive harsh environmental launches.
* **Form Factor Options:**
  * **Option A (CanSat Framework):** Uses a structural skin made from a recycled aluminum soda can. Inside, a vertical column of circular internal shelves (cardboard/acrylic) organizes the layout—positioning the heavy battery at the base for a stable center of gravity, with the NodeMCU in the middle and the sky-facing GPS antenna at the top.
  * **Option B (3D-Printed 1U CubeSat Framework):** A standardized $10\text{cm} \times 10\text{cm} \times 10\text{cm}$ rigid skeleton frame. Employs a multi-story stack of perforated prototype circuit boards secured via vibration-proof M3 nylon standoffs.
* **Thermal Mitigation:** Because high-altitude balloon flights reach the stratosphere where temperatures plummet to $-50^\circ\text{C}$, the inner hull walls must be heavily lined with Styrofoam or specialized bubble wrap insulation. This traps the internal ambient heat generated by the NodeMCU processor, preventing the LiPo batteries from freezing and experiencing catastrophic voltage drops.

### Phase 5: High-Altitude Balloon (HAB) Near-Space Launch

* **Objective:** Execute a localized launch into the Earth's stratosphere (up to 20-40 km altitude) to test systems under true space-like conditions.
* **Launch Logistics:** The completed insulated satellite container is rigged to a heavy-duty nylon recovery parachute, which is tethered to a large latex weather balloon filled with helium or hydrogen gas.
* **Flight Lifecycle:** The balloon expands due to dropping external air pressure until it reaches its volumetric bursting point in near-vacuum conditions. The parachute deploys automatically in the thin air to stabilize the descent. Ground tracking is executed via localized Wi-Fi searching (short-range) and automated GSM SMS transmission (long-range) to recover the physical hardware.

### Phase 6: Transitioning to Rocket Engineering (The Launch Vehicle)

* **Objective:** Move from high-altitude static drifting into dynamic, guided rocket propulsion.
* **Logistics & Material Framework:** Convert the satellite's role into an internal payload and shift focus to the vehicle.
* **Control Systems Integration:** The previously held **KK2.1 flight controller board** (which contains a built-in Atmel processor, an LCD screen, and an independent MPU6050 IMU) is integrated directly into the rocket’s mid-section.
* **Guidance Systems:** The KK2.1 flight controller is flashed with active stabilization algorithms. It links up to internal motorized servo motors to control active aerodynamic fins or a gimbaled engine mount, allowing the rocket to dynamically adjust its trajectory against wind shear during flight.
* **Propulsion Analysis:** Transition from model solid rocket powders to evaluating liquid fuel propulsion mechanics, prioritizing liquid engines for final stages to achieve the engine throttling, precise orbital timing, and specific impulse efficiency necessary to insert payloads into precise paths.
